# TRIBE_SALES_ANALYSIS_PROJECT

# Description

  📌 Description

> This Excel-based project analyzes Prosper Tribe’s sales data to uncover insights related to product performance, sales channels, revenue distribution, and customer activity. The case study focuses on transforming raw sales data into meaningful insights that can support strategic business decisions.

> Key features include data cleaning, Pivot Tables, visual charts, and an interactive dashboard built entirely in Microsoft Excel.

# Project Overview

> This project analyzes Prosper Tribe’s sales data using Microsoft Excel to identify top-performing products, sales channels, and revenue patterns through Pivot Tables and dashboard visualizations.

# Tools Used

Microsoft Excel (Excel functions, Pivot Tables, Charts & Dashboard visualizations)

# Key Business Questions Answered

> Which products generate the highest total revenue?

> Which sales channels contribute most to overall sales?

> How is revenue distributed across different products?

> Are there products with high sales volume but low revenue?

> What overall sales patterns and trends can be observed?


# Key Findings

# Product Performance

>     A small number of products contribute a significant portion of total revenue.

>     Some products show low revenue contribution, indicating underperformance.

>     Revenue concentration suggests reliance on top-selling products.

# Sales Channel Performance

>     Certain sales channels outperform others in terms of revenue.

>     Lower-performing channels present opportunities for optimization.

>     Channel performance varies significantly across products.

# Revenue Distribution

>     Revenue is unevenly distributed across products and channels.

>     High-performing combinations drive overall business results.

>     Product diversity exists, but revenue dependency is concentrated.

# Conclusion

>  The analysis shows that Prosper Tribe’s revenue is driven primarily by a small set of high-performing products and sales channels. While these segments generate most of the revenue, several products and channels underperform, presenting opportunities for strategic improvement.

>  Focusing on optimizing underperforming areas while strengthening top performers can lead to improved overall sales performance.
